# Assumptions and Pressure Test

## Load-Bearing Assumptions

### 1. Reusable data exists at the right level

Assumption: the `languageR::dative` dataset is reusable at token level with
enough predictors to reproduce the original-style analysis.

Falsification condition: the dataset is only a teaching extract, lacks the
critical predictors, has licensing restrictions that block reuse, or differs
from the published target in ways that make "replication" misleading.

Fallback: frame the project as a reproducible reanalysis of a canonical teaching
dataset inspired by Bresnan et al., and make the original chapter the conceptual
target rather than the replication target.

### 2. Cross-corpus generalization is empirical, not decorative

Assumption: later dative datasets are similar enough in outcome definition and
metadata to test whether the conditioning profile travels across corpus design,
modality, time, or variety.

Falsification condition: the later datasets define the alternation or predictors
in incompatible ways, or the corpus differences are so large that every failed
generalization is uninterpretable.

Fallback: make comparability itself the result: what has to be held fixed before
an alternation profile can be transported?

### 3. Modern modelling improves the claim

Assumption: mixed-effects, Bayesian, regularized, or cross-validated updates add
something more than a technical restatement of the original model.

Falsification condition: the modern models produce the same substantive
conclusion without changing uncertainty, generalization, lexical effects, or the
interpretation of grammatical possibility.

Fallback: keep the statistical update modest and foreground the conceptual
lesson about production probability and grammaticality.

### 4. Acceptability resources belong in this paper

Assumption: DAIS or related acceptability resources can connect production
choice to acceptability preference without changing the subject.

Falsification condition: the acceptability datasets force a second paper about
judgment methodology, or they measure a different construct too indirectly.

Fallback: mention acceptability resources as future work and keep this paper
focused on production-choice evidence.

## Unstated Assumptions To Watch

- A "classic update" is only useful if the original paper's empirical question
  remains live. Check whether the dative literature has already absorbed this
  update.
- A no-new-data paper still needs a new inferential contribution. Re-running a
  model is not enough.
- "Grammatical possibility" must be defined before the statistics can bear on
  it. Otherwise the paper will slide between production frequency, preference,
  acceptability, and theoretical grammar.
- Lexical effects are not nuisance by default. They may be part of the
  grammatical profile the paper is trying to understand.

## Anti-Thesis

The strongest objection is that the project is only a modernized replication of
a well-known corpus model, with no new linguistic claim. The paper succeeds only
if the reanalysis clarifies what sort of evidence production probabilities
provide for grammatical possibility, and where that evidence stops.
