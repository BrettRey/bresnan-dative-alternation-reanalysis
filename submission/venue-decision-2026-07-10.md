## Recommendation (Claude Opus 4.8, 2026-07-10)

Close call between IJCL and Linguistics Vanguard; both have a live conversation
this paper joins. I lean **IJCL**: it removes the AI-policy reception risk that
dominated this session (Benjamins is disclosure-model), its reproducibility
special issue and recent dative-predictability article are the most direct live
conversation, and its reviewer pool is the most assignable for an
external-validation methods paper. The one real cost is a concurrent Reynolds
submission (the DiD paper) already under review at IJCL.

If that optics friction is unwelcome, **Corpora** (Edinburgh UP) is the cleanest
response: IJCL's twin as a disclosure-model, methods-facing venue but with no
concurrent Reynolds submission, trading only reach and the closeness of the
methods thread. **Linguistics Vanguard** stays a defensible primary (2024
alternations special issue; short-format fit; package largely built) if the
AI-prohibition reception risk is accepted with a truthful disclosure, and
**Glossa/OLH** is the values-aligned option if AI-policy reception is the deciding
factor, contingent on a grammatical-possibility-forward reframe.

So the decision hinges on one question: are you comfortable with two concurrent
Reynolds submissions at IJCL? If yes, IJCL for reach. If no, Corpora is the clean
methods pick, and the two are otherwise near-equivalent.
